The Long Decline

A 60-point drop in three decades

From hosting 4-in-5 global trial sites in the mid-1990s, the US now accounts for just 1-in-5. Key regulatory reforms and cost pressures made the migration structural — and self-reinforcing.

The New Giants

China absorbed what America ceded

The declining US share wasn't redistributed evenly. China and India captured the majority — driven by vast patient populations, lower costs, and aggressive regulatory liberalization.

The Economics

The US costs 10× more
per trial patient

Running a Phase 3 trial with 3,000 patients costs ~$124M in the US. The same trial in India costs under $15M. With pharma R&D budgets under pressure, the math became unavoidable.

Not All Phases Are Equal

Phase 3 went global.
Phase 1 still calls home.

Early-phase trials need specialized labs and stay anchored to the US and Western Europe. Phase 3 pivotal trials — which need thousands of patients — have gone decisively international.

Where We Are Now

America still leads in discovery.
Not in execution.

The US still originates the majority of new drug candidates and leads in Phase 1 safety work. But the pivotal large-scale trials that prove drugs work — and shape the regulatory fate of a medicine — are increasingly run elsewhere.
