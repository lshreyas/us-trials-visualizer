A Data Story · 1995 — 2024

The US still invents most new drugs.
The world tests them.

Of every 500 industry trial sites active worldwide today, only 100 are in the United States — even though US pharma still originates the majority of new drug candidates. Each square below represents one trial site, the hospital or clinic where a study is being conducted; a single Phase 3 study can run at hundreds of sites across dozens of countries. What Changed

About 80% of trial sites are in the United States.

The 1992 Prescription Drug User Fee Act (PDUFA) accelerated FDA reviews, concentrating pharma research at home. China and India did not yet have the regulatory infrastructure to host major international trials.

The Bigger Pie

More people get drugs tested for them than ever in history.

Total participants in industry-sponsored trials grew from roughly 2.5 million in 1995 to about 40 million today. The "American decline" partly reflects a denominator that doubled, then doubled again — humanity gained unprecedented access to experimental therapies, even as the US share shrank.

Per capita, the US still leads. An American is 2.6× more likely than a Chinese citizen to live near an active trial site — and 6× more likely than someone in India. The shift is real. The framing matters.

The Discovery–Execution Split

Phase 3 went global. Phase 1 stayed home.

Early-phase studies need specialized labs, complex monitoring, and small cohorts of carefully chosen patients — that work stays anchored to the US and Western Europe. Late-phase pivotal trials, which need thousands of patients to prove a drug works, have gone decisively international.

The Economics

A US trial patient costs nearly 7× more than one in China.

Running a 3,000-patient Phase 3 study costs about $124 million in the United States — and about $19 million in China. The US is also slower: enrolling 1,000 patients takes nearly three years here, but just one year in China.

The Pattern

Discovered in America. Tested elsewhere.

Three forces moved the trial map: regulatory reforms abroad (China's 2015 CFDA overhaul, India's 2005 Patents Act, the EU's 2014 Clinical Trials Regulation), the cost of running a US trial site, and the size of patient populations across Asia. Most new drugs still originate in American labs. But the trials that decide whether they work are increasingly run almost everywhere else.
