What Changed

About 80% of trial sites are in the United States.

The 1992 Prescription Drug User Fee Act (PDUFA) accelerated FDA reviews, concentrating pharma research at home. China and India did not yet have the regulatory infrastructure to host major international trials.

Three Decades, Three Drivers

A reshaped industry — by regulation, cost, and patient access.

Three forces moved the trial map: regulatory reforms abroad (China's 2015 CFDA overhaul, India's 2005 Patents Act, the EU's 2014 Clinical Trials Regulation), rising trial costs in the US, and large untreated patient populations across Asia. The industry still discovers drugs in America. It increasingly tests them elsewhere.
